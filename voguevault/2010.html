Timeline

2010



Hipster Heaven

The hipster subculture reached its peak, with vintage and thrifted clothing, flannel shirts, skinny jeans, and beanies defining the aesthetic. Accessories like oversized glasses, satchels, and statement scarves were staples. Indie bands and coffee shop culture inspired the vibe, as did the rise of eco-friendly brands promoting sustainability.



Rise of Festival Fashion

Events like Coachella drove festival fashion into mainstream culture. Crochet tops, bohemian maxi dresses, flower crowns, and fringe bags became synonymous with music festivals. The carefree, boho-chic aesthetic was fueled by brands like Free People, Anthropologie, and Urban Outfitters. Glitter makeup and temporary metallic tattoos added a playful edge to these looks.



High-Waisted Everything

From jeans to shorts and skirts, high-waisted silhouettes took over. Inspired by retro styles, these pieces were often paired with crop tops to create flattering, hourglass silhouettes. Denim brands like Levi’s and Topshop led the charge, while high-waisted swimwear became a beach essential.





The Galaxy Print Craze

From leggings to backpacks, galaxy prints were everywhere in the early 2010s. The cosmic aesthetic turned space-inspired fashion into a phenomenon, fueled by Tumblr trends and pop culture’s fascination with the universe. It was a literal out-of-this-world moment for fashion!
